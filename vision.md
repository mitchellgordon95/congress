# Congress Chat — Vision & Strategy

**One-liner:** Make it easy and fun to find out what's happening in Congress (and eventually your statehouse and city hall) using AI to transform C-SPAN-like sources into consumable form.

## Problem

The decisions that shape law at every level of U.S. government happen in long, boring meetings and dense records that almost nobody — especially younger people — can realistically consume. The information is public but not *consumable*. (Mitchell, 2026-07-20.)

## Product Direction

Working ideas (see `wiki/main.md` for the live brainstorm):

- A chat-thread experience where representatives appear as chat personas; notable floor activity arrives as push messages with click-through, bite-sized breakdowns.
- Vote events: who voted yes/no and why.
- Topic subscriptions and alerts.
- Possible umbrella with the Outrage project (rep responsibility + contact tools): "using AI to improve civic participation."

## Scope

U.S. Congress first; state and local governments are the interesting long-term extension.

## Not a Business

Explicitly non-commercial (Mitchell, 2026-07-20). Success metric is civic value, not revenue. Corollary: don't duplicate others' good work — integrate or point to it.

*(TBD — first audience: civics educators? journalists? politics enthusiasts? Depends partly on landscape research.)*

## Hypotheses

- Chat/feed format materially increases how much civic information people actually consume, vs. transcripts and articles.
- AI summarization can be faithful enough to the record to be trustworthy, and faithfulness is verifiable.
- Consumption (this project) and action (Outrage) are stronger paired than separate.
